Head and Neck Cancer—Patient Version

Health Professional Version

Overview

Head and neck cancers include the larynx, throat, lips, mouth, nose, and salivary glands. They usually begin in squamous cells, the cells that line moist surfaces such as those inside the mouth, nose, and throat. Because salivary glands have many different types of cells, there are several types of salivary gland cancer.

Tobacco use, heavy alcohol use, and infection with human papillomavirus (HPV) increase the risk of head and neck cancer.

Knowing the exact type and stage of head and neck cancer is important to get the right information about treatment and prognosis.
